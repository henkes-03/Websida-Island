Topplista Jullåtar

Icelanders have several unique holidays and celebrate the more common ones in special ways. Many of the festivities are related to ancient Norse traditions, while others tie in to the Christian calendar, even though nowadays most Icelanders celebrate these events in a secular way. In addition to the traditional holidays listed here, numerous festivals take place throughout the year.


Sumardagurinn fyrsti - Celebrates the first day of summer, the third Thursday in April.


Easter - More or less the same as the Swedish easter. You get chocolate eggs filled with sweets. Most kids when they are 14 years old get confirmation during easter.


Twelfth Night - This is the last day of Christmas you celebrate in Iceland. This takes place on the 6th of January. You celebrate this by bonfires and different fireworks.


National day June 17th - This is a big celebration for the Icelandic inhabitants. They meet together in parades in each city. Walking along the streets with a brass band leading the parades is a common way to celebrate this day.


New year - This is a big celebration in Iceland. They celebrate new year with a lot of fireworks and have gotten famous for this. Nearly everyone in Iceland does follow the same New Year routine. First they shoot a lot of fireworks and they party for many hours in glistering outfits and snow boots.
